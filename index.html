About

A passionate programmer and game designer with years of experience teaching video game programming, Vanessa has trained students in both the fundamentals of object-oriented programming and the best practices of development in game engines. She is confident in both leading roles and roles following leadership, having experience in a range of positions. She is flexible and willing to step up to any role, from character programming, to UX design, to even technical art and animation.

Projects

Deck-Building Fighting Game

This project is a spiritual successor to Spire, the previous fighting game project, redesigned from the ground up in Unreal Engine 5 as a deck-driven fighting game that combines elements of fencing and tarot. My role on the project is programming, technical animation, UX design, and card design. The game has yet to be announced in full, but my contributions are visible in the public repository linked.

DOGGONE

DOGGONE is a Unity game from Raconteur Games. I primarily programmed items from the player character to level systems, as well as shaders.

The Legend of Excalipurr

The Legend of Excalipurr is a Wisp Entertainment retro action adventure title for which I was the programmer and designer. It was made in Game Maker Studio 2. See the blog for design details. A demo is available on Steam.

Spire

Spire was an Unreal Engine 4 class-based fighting game where players would build a team and create effects that persisted through the battle. On this project I was the programmer and technical artist.

Teaching Examples

Maze Chaser

Maze Chaser is a simplistic Unity example for students using no imported assets. It is playable using mouse, keyboard, gamepad, or touch screen.

AIE UX Lecture

I wrote a quick primer on some UX considerations for students to consider when developing their projects during my time at AIE.

Flappy Bird Tutorial
